Towards speaker characterisation using Neural TTS(Text to Speech) voices

Abhinav Bhardwaj and Sai Sirisha Rallabandi

Quality and Usability Lab

Technical University Berlin

Abstract:

With the advent of computing abilities, the Text-to-Speech (TTS) community has achieved high quality speech synthesis in the recent past. However, the existing speech synthesisers still lack the human-ness in many ways. This refers to the additional information such as age, gender, emotional state of the person, to name a few. All this information is collectively called the extra-linguistic or the paralinguistic information. Now that we have achieved very good speech synthesis, the research community is focussed on delivering this paralinguistic information along with the speech through the synthesisers. Nevertheless, there is still a significant research gap in terms of evaluating the synthesisers that are developed for different application domains. Through our work, we investigate the perceptual dimensions contributing to the social speaker characteristics warmth and competence in the generated speech. We perform this study on various Neural end-to-end TTS systems. Therefore, through this project, we will explore domain-specific perceptual acceptance of state-of-the art TTS systems. For its study, we would focus on the application domains, health care and customer service.
